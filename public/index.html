e-Laya

Nobody should disappear inside the justice system — least of all the people waiting in it.

e-Laya is a module for the eGovPH super app. It carries information the last few metres — from the institutions that already hold it, to the people whose lives depend on it: a mother who cannot visit this week, a lawyer with three hundred clients, a social worker with a paper register, and a child whose age nobody can confirm.

"Walang dapat mawala sa sistema."

89%

of people held in our jails have not been convicted of anything. They are waiting — and waiting is an information problem.

92,084

people were released last year through the work of BJMP paralegals. The system already works. What fails is telling anyone in time.

1 visit

is the only way a family currently learns how their relative is doing. For many that is four hours of travel and a day's lost wage.

2013

is when the law required a national record system for children in conflict with the law. It was never built.

Six ways in, one system

The people who need this most are the least likely to own a phone — so access is split. Every surface signs in through eGovPH, and each one shows only what that person is permitted and equipped to see.

Family app

Counsel's caseload

For Public Attorney's Office lawyers
The problem

A PAO lawyer may carry hundreds of clients at once. The cases that go wrong are rarely the loud ones — they are the hearing that was held and never written down, and the client who became eligible for release while nobody was looking.

What they can do
  • Open to the cases that need attention today, not an alphabetical list
  • See a client's full history, hearings and programme progress in one place
  • Record an outcome and notify the family in the same action

Programme attendance

For social workers and programme providers
The problem

Diversion and rehabilitation attendance lives on paper. A child who quietly stops attending — or who is attending perfectly but has been given too few sessions to finish in time — is only discovered at a review months later.

What they can do
  • Log a full session in seconds, on a phone, even with no signal
  • See who needs a follow-up — and who cannot finish on the current schedule
  • Issue an attendance record that anyone can independently verify

/verifyworking

Sino siya? · Identity resolution

For LSWDO, PAO, and BJMP / BuCor records officers
The problem

The justice system holds people it cannot name. A child booked as an adult loses the diversion the law guarantees them. An adult booked under an alias loses everything downstream of a matched record — the family cannot find them, the court and jail files never join, release paperwork stalls.

What they can do
  • Interview the person conversationally, in their own language, with "hindi ko alam" always a valid answer
  • Reconcile whatever they remember against civil registration — a human confirms, never the assistant
  • Derive age at the incident date, and route to RA 9344 or adult intake from one flow
  • Refer the unregistered to late registration or foundling recognition — a path to existing in the record

How it connects to eGovPH

e-Laya holds no case file and no custody register of its own. It is a layer over services that already exist — which is why it can be small, and why it does not ask any agency to migrate anything.

eGovPH serviceWhat it does here
eGov SSOEstablishes who is asking and what their role permits them to see. It is also the lawful basis for handling any of this information at all.
eVerifyReads a National ID the person hands over, to confirm identity and — where a child's future depends on it — age.
Face LivenessConfirms identity at the terminal for someone who no longer holds any ID.
eMessageDelivers every notification by SMS, so a family is reached on a basic phone with no internet.
eGov AIRuns the terminal in nine Philippine languages and reads any screen aloud.
eReport (PSGC)Supplies the official barangay hierarchy, so "a programme near me" means a real, matchable place.
Compass (DBM)Provides published national budget figures, used to ground our cost estimates in real numbers.

What we do not claim. The courts, BJMP, BuCor, DSWD and PAO each hold data e-Laya would eventually read from, and no public interface to those systems exists today. Every screen here is therefore built on sources that already exist — a document in someone's hand, a National ID they present, and records the module itself creates. The day those agency connections open, they attach behind an interface that already works.
